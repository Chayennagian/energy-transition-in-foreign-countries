Did you know that Morocco has the largest solar power plant project in the world?

Morocco is located in the North-West of Africa. More than 80% of Morocco’s electricity is generated using fossil fuels, while renewable energy accounted for nearly all of the remainder, which was very little: wind 9%, hydroelectricity 5%, and solar 1%.

The Moroccan government plans to reduce its dependence on foreign imports by developing renewable energy, such as solar plants, to meet its domestic electricity needs.

Morocco is working on a project called the Noor Power Plant. As the world’s largest solar plant power project, it is located in an area with a lot of sun. Quite clever!

Noor Power Plant

Morocco’s Noor Power Plant is the largest concentrated solar power project in the world. It is located in the city of Ouarzazate, in the district of Agadir, Morocco.

With the help of the German Federal Environment Ministry, 15 million euro will be spent on the Noor Power Plant. The power plant will generate 370 gigawatts of electrical energy and reduce CO2 emissions by 230 tonnes each year.

This project is located in a very suitable region with one of the highest amounts of sunlight in the world. It will initially supply 650,000 local residents with solar electricity from dawn until three hours after sunset.

By 2030, the percentage of installed electricity generation capacity based on renewable energies is planned to reach 52%. In addition to solar energy, the country is also focusing on wind and waterpower. That means that only 28% of electricity will come from fossil fuels instead of the 80% that the country is currently generating from fossil fuels.

Way to go Morocco!!

How does it work?

  • A solar panel consists of solar cells. They are usually made of silicon, that only conducts electricity when light falls on it.

  • As soon as the sun shines on the panel, an electric current is created between the positive charge on top of the cell and the negative charge below. That’s called direct current.

  • An inverter converts this current into alternating current: the same type of current that comes from your plug connection.

  • The inverter sends the power into the electricity grid, ready to be used.

  • Solar panels are quite an investment. Depending on how many panels fit on your roof, investing in this energy option may cost a few thousand euros. But with ten panels you can quickly save around 500 euros per year on your energy costs.
