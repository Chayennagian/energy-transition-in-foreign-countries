Did you know that the project ‘OSOWOGG’ a.k.a. 'One Sun, One World and One Grid global' will be the first international network of global interconnected solar power grid?

The power sector in India has undergone significant progress after its Independence. When India became independent in 1947, the country had a power generating capacity of 1,362 MW. Hydro power and coal-based thermal power have been the main sources of generating electricity.

These days India generates most of its electricity from solar power plants. India has a lot of these spread around the country. The solar plants in India belong among the biggest solar power plants in the world!!

Currently India is working on a project to make an international network of solar power grid.

One Sun, One World and One Grid global

Due to its affordability and accessibility, solar energy is being recognized by scientists as a substitute for other sources due to its important component of renewable energy. However, the sun is only visible during the day and, not or hardly visible when there is bad weather. How does this work?

OSOWOG is the solution to this challenge. “One Sun, One World and One Grid global” is part of the International Solar Alliance (ISA), of which India and other countries are members of. Its purpose is to provide clean and low-cost solar power in solar-rich nations like India and other countries around the equator to make them self-reliant and energy secure.

The main thought behind the project OSOWOG, was that

“The sun never sets”

The concept is to develop a worldwide grid through which clean energy can be transmitted anywhere and anytime because it uses power at night in one part of the world from solar energy generated on other side of the world where it is daytime. Moreover, a global grid will ensure a 24-hour energy supply by sharing energy with other countries around the globe.

How does it work?

  • A solar panel consists of solar cells. They are usually made of silicon, that only conducts electricity when light falls on it.

  • As soon as the sun shines on the panel, an electric current is created between the positive charge on top of the cell and the negative charge below. That’s called direct current.

  • An inverter converts this current into alternating current: the same type of current that comes from your plug connection.

  • The inverter sends the power into the electricity grid, ready to be used.

  • Solar panels are quite an investment. Depending on how many panels fit on your roof, investing in this energy option may cost a few thousand euros. But with ten panels you can quickly save around 500 euros per year on your energy costs.

The OSOWOGG project

  • 24-hour energy supply by sharing energy with other countries.
  • Low-cost solar power due to its affordability and accessibility.
  • Solar energy is an important component for renewable energy.
  • A lot of countries can benefit from the international grid.
  • It may be difficult to maintain a stable grid over a large geographical area. An electricity grid is vulnerable to weather, accidents and cyber-attacks.
  • It will be challenging to cover the cost-sharing because this project wil cost a lot and the countries participating are poor and rich.
